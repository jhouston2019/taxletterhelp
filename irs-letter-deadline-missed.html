IRS Letter Deadline Already Passed – What to Do Now

Missing an IRS deadline is serious but not irreversible. The appropriate response depends on which notice you missed and how much time has passed. Act immediately to minimize consequences.

What Happens When You Miss an IRS Deadline

Consequences depend on the notice type:

Missed CP14/CP501/CP503 Deadline

  • The debt remains and interest continues to accrue
  • You will receive an escalated notice (CP503 or CP504)
  • You can still arrange payment or payment plan

Missed CP2000 Deadline

  • The IRS may issue a statutory notice of deficiency
  • Proposed changes may become final
  • You may still submit a late response with explanation
  • If already final, you may need to file an amended return or pay the assessment

Missed CP504/LT11/CP90 Deadline (CRITICAL)

  • Levy action may have already begun or is imminent
  • IRS can garnish wages, levy bank accounts, or seize property
  • You may have lost your right to a Collection Due Process hearing
  • Contact the IRS immediately and engage professional representation

Immediate Actions to Take

Do These Things Today

  • Identify the exact notice type and original deadline
  • Calculate how many days past the deadline you are
  • Call the IRS phone number on the notice immediately
  • Explain you missed the deadline and ask what options remain
  • Submit a written response immediately, referencing the missed deadline
  • If levy notice, request an equivalent hearing if Collection Due Process deadline has passed

Can You Still Respond After the Deadline?

Yes, in most cases a late response is better than no response:

  • Balance Due Notices – You can still pay or request a payment plan at any time
  • CP2000 (Income Mismatch) – Late responses may be accepted if you provide reasonable cause for the delay
  • Levy Notices – You may request an equivalent hearing or appeal if you have not already received a Collection Due Process hearing
  • Audit Notices – Contact immediately to reschedule or explain the missed deadline

Penalty Abatement for Missed Deadlines

If you missed a deadline due to reasonable cause, you may request penalty abatement:

  • Serious illness or family emergency
  • Death in immediate family
  • Natural disaster
  • Unavoidable absence
  • IRS error or delay

Submit Form 843 (Claim for Refund and Request for Abatement) with supporting documentation.
