IRS Letter Types We Handle

What is a CP2000 Notice?

A CP2000 notice means the IRS found a discrepancy between the income reported on your tax return and information they received from third parties. This common notice requires a careful response within 30 days to avoid additional penalties and interest. Our AI analyzes your specific CP2000 and generates the appropriate response.

Common IRS Letters Explained

  • CP2000: Proposed changes to your tax return (income discrepancy)
  • CP2501: Request for information about income
  • 1099-K: Payment card and third-party transactions
  • CP3219A: Statutory notice of deficiency
  • Letter 525: General 30-day letter
  • Letter 566: Initial contact for examination
